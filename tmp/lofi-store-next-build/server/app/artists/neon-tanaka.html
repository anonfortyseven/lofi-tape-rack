About

Born Yuki Tanaka in the neon-lit backstreets of Shibuya, Neon Tanaka spent his youth between his father's jazz kissaten and the arcades of Akihabara. A classically trained pianist who discovered MPC sampling during a gap year in New York, he returned to Tokyo with a vision: marry the warmth of 70s Japanese jazz with the crunch of golden-era hip-hop drums. His debut tape 'City Rain' caught fire on Japanese YouTube, racking up millions of streams from students pulling all-nighters. Now 28, he produces from a cramped apartment overlooking the Yamanote Line, often recording the train announcements that pepper his tracks. Known for his perfectionist tendencies—he reportedly made 47 versions of his track 'Shibuya Crossing' before release.

Signature Sound

Warm jazz piano chords over crispy SP-404 drums with city rain samples, train sounds, and convenience store door chimes

Visual Aesthetic

Neon-soaked cityscapes with anime influences. Heavy use of purple, cyan, and magenta. Rain-slicked streets, convenience store glows, train platform silhouettes. Inspired by 80s city pop album covers meets Blade Runner aesthetics.
