About

Marcus 'Dusty' Summers grew up in his uncle's record shop in Leimert Park, LA's historic Black arts district. Surrounded by crates of forgotten soul, funk, and R&B records, he developed an encyclopedic knowledge of samples before he could drive. At 16, he started flipping obscure 45s on a beat-up MPC 2000 in the shop's back room. His production style is deceptively simple—find the perfect four-bar loop, add crackle, let it breathe—but his ear for soul is unmatched. Now 32, Dusty has become something of a curator-producer, his tapes serving as both original compositions and guided tours through forgotten corners of Black American music. He still works from the same back room, though he owns the shop now.

Signature Sound

Dusty soul samples with heavy vinyl crackle, boom-bap drums, subtle bass, and perfectly-placed record scratches

Visual Aesthetic

Vintage photography and collage. Faded Polaroids, 70s album cover aesthetics, record store snapshots. Warm sepia tones, burnt orange, mustard yellow, and cream. Worn paper textures, coffee stains, handwritten labels.
